1Chapter 23. Pressure Sensors and Load Cells

2Pressure sensors 12251are an evolution of the simple button. 12252 A simple button has two states, on or off, which can be used to trigger simple atomic actions in a video game such as firing a gun or opening a door. 12253 However, simple buttons are not capable of informing the program how you, the user, hits that button. 12254 Did you hit it quickly? 12255 Did you barely touch it at all? 12256 The only thing the program can interpret is that you did in fact hit the button.

11With pressure sensors, the program has the ability to discern 11how12259 the user pressed the button. 12260 This information can be used as incremental input, such as the player raising a firearm before pressing the button harder to actually fire. 12261 Additionally, pressure sensors can be used to create novel forms of human-input devices. 12262 While pressure sensitivity is not uncommon in the more traditional console gaming markets, there is also a recent push to move the sensors into touch-screen devices like the Nintendo DS and cell phone gaming market. 12263 Pressure-sensitive touch screens are currently beyond state of the art, however, so we'll primarily discuss the traditional methods already in widespread adoption.

18In addition to pressure sensors, some new gaming consoles 20use 20load cells12268 to allow the player to use shifts in his or her body weight as input. 12269 The method by which this data is collected and how the center of gravity is determined will be discussed in this chapter. 22 Lastly, some smartphones now include 23a 23barometer12272 , a pressure sensor that measures the pressure of the atmosphere. 12273 What it is used for and the type of information it can provide will also be discussed.

28Under Pressure

30As discussed in 31Chapter 331, 31pressure31 is a 12281force applied over an area. 12282 Imagine a concrete block sitting on a steel plate. 12283 The weight of the block will be evenly distributed over the area of contact, creating a pressure on the steel plate. 12284 Gas and liquid can apply pressure as well. 37 The weight of the air pressing down on us is what is known 39as 39atmospheric12287 pressure.

12290Let's cover a quick example of how to calculate pressure just to illustrate the concepts involved. 43 12293Pressure has many different units, but all of them can be equated to a force divided by an area. 12294 For this chapter we'll stick with Newtons per square meter, as this is easiest to visualize. 47 The SI derived unit (a unit of measure made up of other fundamental units) is called a Pascal, which is just 1 N/m47212295 .

95Button Mashing

97While the 12347preceding example highlights some important concepts about pressure in general, it is not usually the type of pressure used as input to a game. 12348 The most common types of pressure sensors you'll experience in video games are pressure-sensitive buttons that indirectly measure the amount of pressure the user is exerting on the button and convert this to a relative value. 101 Both Sony and Microsoft have incorporated pressure-sensitive (also known as 101analog12349 ) buttons into their controllers for the PlayStation and Xbox series of consoles.

12352The method by which you can detect how hard a user is pressing a button varies from very simple to very complex. 12353 We'll focus on Sony's method, which is very elegant. 12354 A typical push button is just two contacts separated by an insulator, most commonly air. 12355 When the button is pushed, the upper contact moves down and touches the lower contact. 12356 This completes a circuit, causing a voltage spike, which the device interprets as a button press. 12357 This is another example of a digital sensor—it is either on or off. 12358 The buttons in Sony's controller work a bit differently. 111 In State A in 112Figure 23-212361 , we can see that the button is not yet pressed and an air gap exists between the solid conductor and the domed flexible conductor. 12362 In State B the button is depressed with minimal pressure, and the dome just barely makes contact. 12363 The button is now activated. 12364 If the user continues to press down harder on the button, the dome deflects and increases the area of contact with the fixed conductor; the larger the contact area, the greater the conductivity of the connection. 12365 This causes a rise in the current flowing in the circuit.

123Figure 23-2. Pressure-sensitive button

12373By measuring this increase in current, the controller knows how far down the button is being pressed. 126 In State D in 127Figure 23-212376 , the button is at its limit of travel and the dome has deflected to its maximum contact area. 12377 The difference between this maximum and the minimum required to detect contact determines the absolute lowest and highest pressure the button is able to differentiate. 130 For instance, let us assume that if the button were depressed completely, the current would register at 130I130max12378 . 12379 If the button were not pressed at all, of course, the current would be 0. 132 If we call the current 132I132(132t132) for any time, 132t132, we see that the ratio 132I132(132t132)/132I132max12380 gives a nondimensional quantity for how far down the button is pressed. 12381 During this operation, the hardware converts the analog voltage to a digital representation suitable for input to a program. 12382 For the Sony example, this value is calculated by the hardware and passed as part of the data stream from the controller with hex values between 0x00 to 0xFF, or in other words, integers 0 to 255 in decimal. 12383 This means that each button's travel is divided into 255 parts that your program can register.

12385While 255 individual increments are beyond the human ability to control fingertip pressure, different ranges of pressure have practical uses in games. 12386 For example, you could program your button to raise a weapon with a half-press (0 to 127), bring the weapon to the shoulder with more pressure (127 to 250), and to fire when totally depressed (250 to 255). 12387 Of course, those values would have to be tuned for the desired level of sensitivity. 12388 Another example would be to control the throttle on a car by using the values of 0 to 255 as thrust multipliers.

142Another use of knowing a button's position 12391would be tracking it over time. 12392 With a time history of position, you can differentiate to get velocity and again to get acceleration. 12393 This would allow the program to differentiate between a button that is either slowly depressed or quickly depressed. 12394 Most hardware doesn't help you here, so you'll have to store the values and calculate the velocities in whatever increments are appropriate for your program. 12395 As real-time velocity sensing might be taxing to the user as real-time input, the best use would be as input to something that the user doesn't have to control constantly. 12396 Imagine having to keep a button pressed down at the correct pressure for your gameplay for longer than a few minutes; I can feel my wrist cramping now. 12397 However, the pressure button is useful for many inputs. 150 For example, how far a button is pressed down might be used to draw back the head of a putter, while the speed at which the button is released could be used to determine the speed at which the putter is brought back to the 12400ball.

155Load Cells

157Beyond simple 12407buttons, there are other novel ways to use pressure sensors to allow a user to interact with your games. 160 For example, Nintendo's Wii uses a balance board peripheral based on load cells to detect a person's 12409stance.

170Tiny scales

172Load cells work 12421differently than the pressure-sensitive buttons described previously, but like pressure-sensitive buttons, they come in different types, all of which measure the load pressing on them. 174 The most common way, and the one used in the Nintendo balance board we'll discuss shortly, is through what is 175called a 175strain gauge12424 .

12427A strain gauge, as you might be able to guess, does not measure force directly but instead measures how much strain the gauge is experiencing. 12428 Strain is a measure of how much a rigid body has deformed independent of its rigid-body motion. 181 While there are several notions of strain in continuum mechanics, the one we are concerned with here is often 182referred to as 182engineering strain12431 . 184 This type of strain quantifies how much a structural element has deformed compared to its original, or 184rest12432 , length. 12433 We normalize this by dividing the change in length over the rest length. 12434 By testing the material, one can develop a stress versus strain curve that relates how much stress it takes to cause a certain amount of strain. 12435 Once the pressure that causes an amount of strain is known, it is possible to determine the amount of load. 12436 Now you might be wondering how the strain gauge measures the amount that the Wii's legs compress when you stand on them.

191One of the most common electronic strain 192gauges is the 192piezoresistive strain gauge12441 . 12442 The simplest example of a piezoresistive strain gauge would be a single wire. 12443 If you were to elongate a wire from its rest length, the cross-sectional area decreases. 12444 This causes a rise in the electrical resistance of the wire. 12445 After measuring the rest resistance, you can use the difference to determine how much the wire has elongated. 12446 Knowing the mechanical properties of the wire, you can also determine how much force it takes to stretch the wire.

201To make strain gauges sensitive without having long linear wire elements, the conductive material is often arranged in a strain-sensitive pattern, as shown in 202Figure 23-312451 . 12452 This looping back and forth of the conductor allows for great sensitivity without increasing the physical space the sensor occupies. 12453 Here the rest length would be 18 times longer than the physical length of the sensor.

360Barometers

362Continuing our 12611exploration of new user input methods, especially in the rapidly maturing mobile device gaming market, now we'll discuss an interesting inclusion in the latest smartphones: a barometer. 12612 Unlike buttons and balance boards, whose pressure sensors only indirectly handle pressure, barometers directly measure the fluid pressure that the atmosphere exerts on the sensor.

367The sensors used in mobile phones today are 368piezoresistive 12619microelectromechanical systems (MEMS) and are very accurate. 372 As shown in 373Figure 23-612622 , the sensors consist of a void machined into a piece of silicon. 12623 The diaphragm is then bonded to a stiff material such as steel or glass. 376 As we are trying to measure 376absolute pressure12624 , this bond is airtight. 377 Using a material called 377monocrystalline semiconductor silicon12625 to form the void ensures that the entire diaphragm acts much like a piezoresistive strain gauge.

383Figure 23-6. MEMS piezoresistive pressure sensor

12633Now we have a situation similar to the steel ball in the ocean, only this time it is a tiny silicon ball in the ocean of air surrounding the earth. 12634 When the sensor is moved deeper or shallower in the atmospheric ocean, the pressure on the outside of the diaphragm changes. 12635 This causes the pressure differential to change and a force to be exerted on the silicon diaphragm. 12636 This force causes a deflection that changes the resistance of the piezoresistive material and can therefore be measured by the sensor. 12637 This part will be taken care of by the hardware and the encoded value sent to the operating system.

392To give you an example, in the Android operating system, the API has a public method, 392getAltitude(float p0, float p)392, to determine altitude, 12641in meters, between the sensor pressure and the pressure at sea level. 394 It usually reads the current 396atmospheric pressure, 396p396, from the sensor by listening to sensor manager callback interface method 396abstract void onSensorChanged(SensorEvent event)12644 . 397 Here the class 397event12645 holds the sensor values, the accuracy of those values, a reference to the sensor itself, and a timestamp for when the event occurred. 398 The pressure is 400reported in hectoPascals (hPa) or 100 N/m400212648 . 401 The sea-level pressure, 401p0401, that this is compared to is either obtained from an online database or is set at the constant 401SensorManager.PRESSURE_STANDARD_ATMOSPHERE12649 . 12650 As pressure at sea level changes with different weather conditions, we obtain higher accuracy by retrieving it from a nearby airport or other weather station via the Internet. 403 To get the change in altitude between two points, you must repeat this process twice as follows:

float altitude_difference = 
getAltitude(SensorManager.PRESSURE_STANDARD_ATMOSPHERE, 
     pressure_at_point2) -
getAltitude(SensorManager.PRESSURE_STANDARD_ATMOSPHERE,
     pressure_at_point1);

12654At first it may seem strange for your cell phone to have a barometer in it; however, the barometer's ability to detect the air pressure allows you to make a good guess on your altitude. 407 As shown in 408Chapter 22408, in order to determine your position via 12659GPS you have to solve a four-dimensional set of linear equations. 12660 The time required to solve these equations can be dramatically decreased if you know approximately where you are to begin with. 12661 Currently, the position of which cell phone tower your phone is connected to is used as a starting point. 12662 Using a barometer allows the device to guess its altitude to further reduce the time to obtain a GPS fix.

12665While the sensor was included for a specific purpose, it can also be adapted as an input device. 12666 For instance, the Bosch BMP180 currently being included in devices is accurate to plus or minus one meter. 419 In fact, Google Maps12668 now provides indoor directions, including knowing what floor you are on in airports and shopping malls. 421 This functionality could be used to aid in the location-based gaming discussed in 422Chapter 2212671 by giving it greater resolution in the vertical dimension. 424 It could also be used to determine if the user is holding the phone near her feet or her head, further augmenting the orientation sensing discussed in 425Chapter 2112674 . 427 Of course, it can also be used to help weather forecasting and allow you to have real-life changes in pressure affect in-game 12676events.
